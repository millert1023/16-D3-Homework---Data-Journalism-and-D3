Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

To visualize the different data view, select one of the headings of the x-axis or one of the headings of the y-axis. The scatter chart will then shift. The circles represent the different States. When a circle is hovered, the specific values for that State is displayed.

Let's dig in and examine the data. Age had little to no correlation when it came to smoking, obesity or healthcare. However, a large majority of States reported smokers, obesity, and a lack of healthcare in 35 to 40 age range.

There is a slight correlation between household income and the various health risks. Household incomes less than $50,000 had greater percentages of smokers, obese people and people reporting a lack of healthcare. .
